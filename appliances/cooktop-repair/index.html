Burner Won't Ignite

LG gas cooktop ignition repair diagnoses burners that won't light when the knob is turned. Gas cooktop ignition requires the igniter to spark while gas flows to the burner—failure of either component prevents ignition. Common causes include wet igniters from boilovers, clogged burner ports, failed spark modules, and gas valve problems. Our technicians systematically diagnose ignition failures throughout Newark, Jersey City, and New Jersey gas cooktop installations.

Weak Flame

LG gas cooktop weak flame repair addresses burners producing insufficient heat for cooking. Weak flames result from clogged burner ports restricting gas flow, improper air-to-gas mixture, low gas pressure, or partially blocked orifices. Food debris and grease buildup commonly restrict gas flow over time. Our technicians clean burner assemblies and adjust gas flow throughout Edison, Woodbridge, and New Jersey.

Clicking But No Ignition

LG gas cooktop clicking repair fixes burners where the igniter clicks repeatedly but flame doesn't establish. The clicking indicates the spark module works but gas isn't reaching the burner or igniting properly. Causes include moisture on the igniter, misaligned burner caps, clogged ports, or gas valve failures. Our technicians identify the specific failure throughout Elizabeth, Trenton, and New Jersey.

Gas Smell Concerns

LG gas cooktop gas odor inspection addresses safety concerns about gas smells near your cooktop. Slight gas odor during ignition is normal as unburned gas briefly escapes before lighting. Persistent gas smell indicates potential leaks requiring immediate attention—strong odor means leave the area and call the gas company. Our technicians perform leak detection throughout Paterson, Clifton, and New Jersey.

Uneven Flame

LG gas cooktop uneven flame repair fixes burners with flames higher on one side or inconsistent around the burner ring. Uneven flames cause hot spots and poor cooking results. Causes include clogged burner ports, warped burner caps, improper cap seating, and burner head damage. Our technicians clean and align burner components throughout Dumont, New Milford, and New Jersey.

Burner Won't Turn Off

LG gas cooktop valve repair addresses dangerous situations where burners continue burning after the knob is turned off. Gas valves stuck open pose fire and safety hazards. Failed valve stems, damaged seals, and control mechanism problems prevent proper shutoff. This requires immediate professional attention. Our technicians repair gas valves throughout New Jersey.

Igniter Replacement

LG gas cooktop igniter replacement installs new spark igniters when existing ones fail. Igniters crack from thermal stress, become coated with debris, or simply wear out over time. Failed igniters produce no spark or weak sparks insufficient for ignition. Our technicians replace igniters with genuine LG parts throughout Tenafly, Cresskill, and New Jersey gas cooktops.

Gas Valve Repair

LG gas cooktop gas valve service repairs or replaces the valves controlling gas flow to each burner. Gas valves regulate flow based on knob position—valve failures cause no gas, continuous gas, or improper flame adjustment. Our technicians service gas valves throughout Wyckoff, Franklin Lakes, and New Jersey gas cooktop installations.

Burner Cap Issues

LG gas cooktop burner cap repair addresses problems with the removable caps distributing flame around the burner. Warped, cracked, or improperly seated caps cause ignition problems and uneven flames. Caps damaged from high heat or impacts need replacement. Our technicians replace burner caps throughout Alpine, Closter, and New Jersey gas cooktops.

Control Knob Problems

LG gas cooktop control knob repair fixes knobs that are loose, broken, or don't properly control the valve. Knob shafts can strip, break, or become difficult to turn. Damaged knobs make flame adjustment difficult or impossible. Our technicians replace control knobs and repair valve stems throughout New Jersey gas cooktop installations.

Gas Line Connections

LG gas cooktop connection service verifies proper gas supply connections to your cooktop. Improper connections cause gas leaks, insufficient supply, or no gas flow. Our technicians inspect gas connections and ensure safe, proper installation throughout New Jersey gas cooktop installations.
